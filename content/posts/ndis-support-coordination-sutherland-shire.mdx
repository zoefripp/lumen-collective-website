Finding the right NDIS Support Coordinator in the Sutherland Shire means finding someone who understands the local area, knows the providers and services available nearby, and has the time to genuinely invest in your situation.

## What a Sutherland Shire Support Coordinator Does

Your Support Coordinator's job is to help you get the most from your NDIS plan. In practical terms, that means:

- Helping you understand what your funding means and what you can use it for
- Connecting you with providers and services in the Sutherland Shire and surrounding areas
- Making sure your supports are set up and working together
- Helping you prepare for your NDIS plan review
- Being available when things change or get complicated

For participants in the Sutherland Shire, local knowledge matters. A coordinator who knows the area can connect you with services more quickly, understands the local provider landscape, and can attend in-person meetings where that's important to you.

## What to Look for in a Local Coordinator

Not all Support Coordinators are the same. When you're looking for a coordinator in the Sutherland Shire, consider:

**Caseload size.** Smaller caseloads mean more time and attention for you. Ask any potential coordinator how many participants they currently support.

**Responsiveness.** A good coordinator returns your calls and messages promptly — not days later. Ask how they communicate and what their typical response time is.

**Experience with your needs.** Whether you're navigating psychosocial disability, complex medical needs, or supporting a child or young person, look for someone with relevant experience.

**Consistency.** Ideally, you'll speak to the same person every time — not a rotating roster of staff.
